Wait But Why, Simplified

The article in 3 minutes

The article says: AI progress may look gradual today, but once systems reach general intelligence, change could become very fast. So society should prepare early, not late.

Stage 1

ANI (today)

  • AI does specific tasks only.
  • Examples: translation, diagnostics, recommendations, customer support.
  • Powerful but narrow.
Stage 2

AGI (future)

  • AI can do many cognitive tasks like a human.
  • Can reason across domains, not just one workflow.
  • Still uncertain when exactly this arrives.
Stage 3

ASI (hypothetical)

  • AI exceeds human capability by a very large margin.
  • Main concern is control, alignment, and governance.
  • Could be hugely beneficial or highly risky.

Core logic of the article

More compute
Better models
Broader capability
Self-improvement loops
Very fast transition risk

Practical takeaway: do not wait for certainty. Build capability, ethics, and public safeguards in parallel.

Simple truth

AI won’t solve everything

Technology can reduce operational scarcity, but social trust, justice, and cohesion still require human institutions.

Simple truth

Progress can be non-linear

Long periods of slow progress can be followed by sudden jumps. Planning must assume discontinuity.

Simple truth

Preparation beats reaction

Skilling, governance, and local deployment models should start now, not after disruption is visible.

IRMA Lens

Why IRMAns are critical in this transition

IRMAns can connect high technology with ground reality. That bridge is the real scarcity in India.

What IRMAns uniquely bring

Rural knowledge: knows field constraints, local power structures, and real demand.

Development practice: can work through NGOs, SHGs, cooperatives, and community networks.

Managerial capability: can run programs with KPIs, budgets, and scale discipline.

What this means in practice

Build voice-first AI services in local languages for last-mile users.

Deploy “human + AI” models where frontline workers stay accountable.

Publish evidence dashboards: turnaround time, grievance closure, error rates, trust scores.

Society

Use AI to widen access to guidance, not replace local dialogue and empathy.

Economy

Use AI to strengthen producer incomes, MSME productivity, and public service efficiency.

Politics

Use AI for transparency and delivery quality, while keeping constitutional institutions central.

“How can AI help when literacy and social division are still serious?”

Thread 1 & 2 • 1 Mar 2026
Is this claim too big?

It becomes credible only with proof. Use measurable pilots and publish results publicly every month.

Can AI end social division by itself?

No. AI can reduce service scarcity and unfairness, but social healing requires ethics, institutions, and leadership.

What about people with limited literacy?

Design voice-first systems in local languages with assisted usage by field workers. “Text-only AI” is not enough for India.

What would count as real progress in 12 months?

Faster grievance resolution, lower service-delivery delays, better targeting of schemes, and higher trust from communities.

Execution

A short action plan for IRMA alumni

Month 1

Form IRMAN AI working groups

Choose domains: agriculture, health, livelihoods, local governance, justice access.

Month 2-3

Run two district pilots

Start small and measurable: one rural district and one urban-poor cluster.

Month 4-6

Open public proof board

Share baseline vs outcome metrics so claims are evidence-based.

Month 7-12

Scale what works

Institutionalize successful workflows through NGOs, state systems, and partner networks.

Rule: no claim without data, no scale without inclusion, no deployment without accountability.
