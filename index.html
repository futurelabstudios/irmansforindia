Systems View

AI, AGI, and ASI: What Changes in Society, Economy, and Politics

Practical Definitions

AI: Narrow systems automating specific tasks (translation, call support, crop diagnostics, fraud detection, service triage).

AGI: A future class of systems capable across many cognitive tasks at near-human generality. Timeline remains uncertain.

ASI: Hypothetical intelligence beyond human capability across most domains; high uncertainty and major governance implications.

Useful stance for IRMAns: act decisively on today’s AI, prepare institutions for AGI-level capability, and insist on safeguards before any ASI-like concentration of power.

Five-Year Transition Logic (2026-2031)

Phase 1: Augmentation

AI copilots improve frontline productivity in health, agriculture, education, and local administration.

Phase 2: Rewiring

Workflows become data-native: fewer paper bottlenecks, faster entitlement delivery, and more auditable decisions.

Phase 3: Redistribution

High-quality guidance becomes cheaper and universal; value shifts from routine processing to trust, coordination, and leadership.

Society

Voice-first local-language AI can reduce exclusion where text literacy is limited. Human mediators remain essential.

Economy

SMEs, producer groups, and cooperatives can gain planning, market intelligence, and process discipline at low cost.

Politics

AI can improve transparency and grievance resolution but cannot replace institutions, law, or ethical leadership.

1. Is this just hype? Where is proof?

Treat all claims as hypotheses until measured. Use a public proof-board: baseline, intervention, outcome, and independent verification. Minimum proof set for any pilot should include service-delivery time, cost per beneficiary, error rate, grievance closure, and trust feedback.

2. If literacy is limited, how can AI be inclusive?

Inclusion cannot rely on text-only tools. Build voice-first, local-language, and assisted interfaces. India’s adult literacy rate is estimated at 81.7% (2023), meaning literacy has improved significantly, but accessibility gaps still require human-supported deployment, especially in vulnerable communities.

3. Can AI alone fix social polarization or communal divides?

No. AI is not a moral substitute for politics, law, or dialogue. What AI can do is reduce scarcity pressure and improve fairness in service delivery. Social cohesion still depends on leadership, institutions, and norms.

4. What tangible outcomes can be delivered this year?
  • District-level multilingual citizen helpdesk with documented closure times.
  • AI support stack for frontline fieldworkers in health/agri/livelihood missions.
  • Farmer-producer and SHG planning assistants for market and scheme decisions.
  • Public audit dashboards tracking error rates, inclusion, and grievance outcomes.
5. Are AGI/ASI discussions premature for a poor country?

Daily governance and livelihoods must remain priority. But institutions should still prepare now for stronger AI capability so governance is not forced to react late. A balanced approach is: immediate AI for delivery, medium-term policy for labor transitions, long-term safety architecture for advanced systems.

Implementation

IRMAN Action Playbook: From Conversation to Outcomes

The transition fails without educated leadership that can work across state, market, and civil society. This playbook is designed for immediate collective execution.

0-30 Days

Constitute Mission Cells

Form domain cells: agriculture, health, education, justice-access, livelihoods, and local governance.

30-90 Days

Run 3 Proof Pilots

Select one district and one urban-poor cluster each. Publish baseline and first outcomes openly.

90-180 Days

Publish Open Playbook v1

Create reusable SOPs, model governance checklists, procurement templates, and training toolkits.

180-365 Days

Scale Through Institutions

Partner with state missions, NGOs, and CSR platforms to expand what demonstrably worked.

One Core Commitment

“No claim without evidence, no scale without inclusion, and no technology without human dignity.”
